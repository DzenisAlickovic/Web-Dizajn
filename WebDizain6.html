Museum "Ras"

The "Ras" Museum in Novi Pazar was founded on June 27, 1952, at a meeting of the Council for Education and Culture of the Municipality of Novi Pazar, called the City Museum in Novi Pazar. It is located in the center of the city, on Zitni trg, in the old Novi Pazar town house of traditional architecture from the middle of the 19th century. The museum is of a complex type and includes the fields of archeology, history, ethnology, numismatics, oriental studies, applied art and contemporary art. It has 333 m2 of exhibition space and 5,876 items, of which the archaeological collection is the most numerous and most processed. In 2013, it was declared an institution of national importance.

Fortress

Novi Pazar Fortress is a medieval Turkish fortress, built by Isa-beg Isahović in the 15th century during the founding of Novi Pazar. It is located in the city center, on the right bank of the Raska and within the city park. The fortification consists of three corner bastions - a bastion and a tower known as the Old Scout, or the Watchtower,located between the northern and western basins. The Motril Tower and the northern bastion are connected by a stone rampart, representing a visible remnant of this type of defensive walls.

Hamam

Isabeg's hamam, also known as the Novi Pazar old hamam, is a public bath in the immediate vicinity of the Old Bazaar, on the right bank of the Raška River in the very center of the Jeni Bazaar in Novi Pazar. The hamam was built by the third fugitive of the Ottoman province of Bosnia and the main founder of Sarajevo and Novi Pazar, Isa-beg Isaković, in the second half of the 15th century. Isabeg's hammam is an example of the so-called of the double city hammam. The building housed men's and women's rooms, completely identical, separated by the longitudinal symmetry of the base and partitioned off by a stone wall. The building has eleven domes of different sizes and with hexagonal openings. Each room is covered with a dome. On the north side of the hammam there was a spacious fountain which was a kind of waiting room and locker room, while on the south side there was a so-called treasury - a room for heating water. There were also three bathing rooms with marble fountains and one bathing break. Today, this monument of Islamic architecture is under the protection of the Republic of Serbia, as a cultural monument of great importance.
